# The DOs :
1. Curate your privacy settings: Regularly audit who can see your posts. High-school-level privacy might not be enough when you start networking for internships.
2. Think before you "Like": Remember that your activity including likes and comments is often visible to others and reflects your associations and values.
3. Network intentionally: Follow professors, industry leaders, and campus organizations. Social media is a powerful tool for finding hidden career opportunities.
4. Fact-check before sharing: As a student, your academic integrity matters. Avoid spreading misinformation by taking 30 seconds to verify a headline.
5. Post your wins: Use your platforms to showcase projects, volunteer work, or club involvements. It acts as a live, informal portfolio for recruiters.

# The DONTs :
1. Don't vent-post about faculty: It’s tempting to complain about a tough professor or a bad grade, but digital footprints are permanent and can be seen as unprofessional.
2. Don't ignore your digital footprint: Avoid posting anything illegal, discriminatory, or excessively Shoreline-at-3-AM. If you wouldn't want it on a billboard, don't post it.
3. Don't engage with trolls: Heated arguments in the comments section rarely end well and can be screenshotted out of context years later.
4. Don't overshare your location: For your physical safety, avoid tagging your exact dorm or current location in real-time. Wait until you've left the venue to post the photo.
5. Don't let the Highlight Reel affect you: Remember that social media often shows the best 1% of people's lives. Don't compare your behind-the-scenes to someone else's curated feed.
